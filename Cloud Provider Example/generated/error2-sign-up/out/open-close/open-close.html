TADL: open-close

after "open" there must be "close": 

CTL AG (open -> AF close)

no multi-open: 

CTL AG (open -> AX A[!open U close])

no multi-close: 

CTL AG (close -> AX !E[!open U (close & !open)])

if there is "close" there must have been a "open" before: 

LTL G (close -> O open)

Temporal Operators Explained

CTL: E[f U g] = 'Exists Until', i.e. exists path where at some point g holds while in the meantime f holds.
CTL: AG(f) = 'All globally', i.e. holds on all paths in all steps.
CTL: A[f U g] = 'All Until', i.e. on all paths at some point g holds while in the meantime f holds.
CTL: AF(f) = 'All finally', i.e. in all paths starting with current step exists a step where f holds.
LTL: G(f) = 'Globally', i.e. f holds in all steps.
CTL: AX(f) = 'All next', i.e. f holds in all following steps.
PLTL: O(f) = 'Once', i.e. f held in at least one step in the past (past also includes current step).
